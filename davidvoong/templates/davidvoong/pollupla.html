Pollupla

Air pollution has been shown to have a long term detrimental impacts on health and well being. By combining air quality data with property market data Pollupla gives potential home buyers and renters the tools to make a better decision on where they live. We hope this information will provide people people with the financial as well as health related incentives to improve the air quality in their neighbourhoods.

Info

The aim of the project is to combine air quality data to the property market so that home owners are motivated to improve the air quality and hence the value of their homes and also providing home buyers with more information when searching for a property. It’s a bit like the energy rating you get for a fridge or washing machine but instead an air pollution rating for a home.

Our thinking was that money will always big a factor when it comes to changing people's/business' behaviour but instead of trying to fight human nature perhaps we can try to leverage it instead.

Here’s a proof of concept my team and I developed, http://pollupla.mybluemix.net/, it was hacked together in one day so don’t expect too much. When you enter a post code it accesses the Zoopla (Zoopla - Pollupla, get it?) database and finds properties in the area, it then collects data from the Kings College Air quality open data network and calculates the pollution at each property. Blue locations means there the data is loading, red, yellow and green locations correspond to the levels of pollution in order from bad to good. It’s a bit slow, so be patient.
