Pollupla

Air pollution has been shown to have a long term detrimental impacts on health and well being. By combining air quality data with property market data Pollupla gives potential home buyers and renters the tools to make a better decision on where they live. We hope this information will provide people with the financial as well as health related incentives to improve the air quality in their neighbourhoods.

How it Works

The pollupla project uses data collected from a network of air quality stations located around central London, the data is collected by and distributed by the King's College's London Air network. This data is combined with data from the Zoopla property database to calculate the air pollution levels for properties near a given postcode.

During the 24 hour Climathon event we put together a prototype, blue markers correspond to properties where the data is being collected and red, yellow and green markers correspond to properties with air quality ratings varying from bad to good (note the queries can be a bit slow, ~15 seconds, so you'll need to be patient).

Climathon

For first prize we were awarded £1000 to develop our business idea which was later increased to €10,000 when we were accelerated along their incubation route, €5000 of which is provided by AMEE, a company aimed at sustainable business.
